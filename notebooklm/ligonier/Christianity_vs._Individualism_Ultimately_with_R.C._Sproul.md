# Christianity vs. Individualism: Ultimately with R.C. Sproul

SPROUL: When we say the Apostles’ Creed, 
we say, “We believe in the communion of the  

saints.” Even though we are indeed individuals, 
Christianity is not a religion of individualism.  

Whenever God deals with me as a person, as a 
private individual, and He redeems my soul,  

privately and individually, He immediately 
places me in a group. He immediately places  

me in a community that the New Testament calls 
the church. And today, the living Jerusalem in  

our lives is to be the visible church, where 
the people of God are gathered in community.
