REEVES: The statistics are striking that young 
children brought up in Christian families are far,  

far more likely to stay strong in the Lord 
if there have been constant family devotions,  

as well as a regular going to church—a 
regular attendance at all the meetings of  

church. And I think it's family devotions 
that in some ways have dropped out of  

our Reformed, Reformation, Puritan 
tradition that used to be very strong.

So, Puritan families used to 
see themselves, in many ways,  

as a micro-church where the father is seeking to 
pastor the family. And so, spending time together,  

actively seeking to ensure that the Word 
is being read aloud, taught and explained,  

and then singing God's praises together and 
praying together, creates a rhythm of devotion  

that is profoundly helpful. It helps embed young 
children, especially, by doing it so constantly;  

it helps them to see how the Gospel applies to 
every part of life. And children can watch you  

as you do devotions with them, as you pray with 
them, praise the Lord with them, read Scripture  

together with them. What they can do is they watch 
how you don't just simply say a few nice Christian  

things to them every now and again; they watch 
how you walk with the Lord through good times and  

through hard times, and how you carry on trusting 
Him. And by having constant devotions with them,  

they're hearing more of the Word of God, and so 
they'll be more rounded in their faith. They'll  

know how to deal with temptation, they'll know 
how to deal with trial, they'll know how to  

deal with doubt, because all these things will 
come up the more you've got those devotions. So,  

I'd like to put in an appeal for a greater 
valuing of regular family devotions, in which  

the Word of God is taught, and read, in which 
the family praises, sings, and prays together.

His light might scatter darkness. And when you  

have that desire, then you're going to be able 
to think creatively for your situation, going,

that's going to be different for every single

Christ is going to mean a love for sharing Him.
