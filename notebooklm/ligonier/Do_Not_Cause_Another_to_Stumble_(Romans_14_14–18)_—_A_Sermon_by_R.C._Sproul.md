bellicose people looking for a fight, contentious 
over every minor point, wanting to fight and

Paul said the kingdom of God’s about 
righteousness. The kingdom of God is  

about a godly peace. And the kingdom of God is not 
made up of a company of sourpusses. Somebody said  

to me at the door today… I told my wife this when 
I got home. I said, “I can’t believe it.” I said,  

“Somebody said to me at the door, they said, 
‘You know what I notice about your preaching,  

RC, is every time you’re going to give a zinger 
to the people right before you give the zinger  

you smile.’” Spoon full of sugar. You know? But 
we should be happy people. The kingdom of God is  

about joy, the joy that has been shed abroad in 
our hearts because we have been redeemed by the  

Lord our God. Why would we get glum and fussing 
over this stuff about who eats meat and who drinks  

wine and all of that stuff? It’s not what the 
kingdom of God is about, Paul is saying. It’s  

about loving the things of God and it’s about 
loving those for whom Christ died. That’s the  

recipe for mature Christian unity.
