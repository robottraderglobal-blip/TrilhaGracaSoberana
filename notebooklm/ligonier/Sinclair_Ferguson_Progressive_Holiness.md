And this is surely the wonderful thing about 
being a Christian. The gospel works everywhere  

and anywhere, because Christ, by His Spirit, 
through His Word, makes all of His children  

like this progressively until we see Him face 
to face, and are made like Him. And when you  

see this in a brother or sister Christian, 
it makes you say, doesn't it? "I'd love to be  

like that." And Paul is saying to these Colossian 
Christians, "By God's grace, you can be like that,  

and through His Spirit, you will be like that, 
and one day you will be perfectly like Him."  
 
 

I wonder what you're going to look like when 
you're perfectly like Him. I wonder how it is that  

I'll recognize you when you're perfectly like him. 
But the one thing we can be sure of is I will be  

able to recognize you, and the reason I'll be able 
to recognize you is because you're so like Him.  

And if that's where it ends, then that's where 
everything between now and then must lead.  
 
 

Let's pray that it will. Let's pray together. 
Lord, we thank you for your Word. We thank you  

for the opportunity in these hours while sitting 
together under it, for the encouragement of being  

together. We thank you for the beauty and the 
simplicity as well as the profundity of the  

gospel, and we pray just now very consciously 
and deliberately, not only for ourselves,  

but for the friend to the right of us or to 
the left of us, that together we may grow in  

our appreciation of what it means to be united to 
the Lord Jesus and encourage one another through  

Your Word and through our singing and the way 
Your truth dwells in us richly, day by day,  

to make progress in becoming more and more like 
the Lord Jesus, that we may encourage each other,  

that we may glorify You, and that we may shine as 
lights in this dark world, to point our friends,  

our family, our neighbors, even those who are 
hostile to us, to our great savior, Jesus Christ,  

and we pray that you will bring this to pass 
more and more in our lives, in Jesus' name. Amen.
