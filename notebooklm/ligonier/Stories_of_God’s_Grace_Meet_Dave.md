# Stories of God’s Grace: Meet Dave

I grew up going to church every Sunday, every 
Wednesday, knew all the right things to say, knew  

all the right things to do. But my god was not 
the God of the Bible. My god was skateboarding.  

You know, I was baptized, I was confirmed, 
and, outside of that, God was an afterthought.  

I was uninterested. God was not important to me. 
The only thing that was important was skating and  

progressing in my skating. As skateboarding 
progressed, I started to pick up sponsorships  

and getting more recognition in the skateboard 
world. I ended up getting sponsored by Tony  

Hawk’s company. I had gone from admiring these 
professionals and all the videos that I’d watch,  

and now I find myself skating with them. Although 
that was my current reality in the skateboard  

world, that I had reached the top, I had still 
this void, and I still was unsatisfied in my  

life. In skateboarding, you’re always trying to 
outdo yourself. You’re trying to do better than  

you did the year before. And you think, “If I 
could just get this one trick, if I could just  

reach this certain level, if I could just get this 
new sponsor, then I’ll be fulfilled, then I’ll be  

happy, then I’ll feel like I’m accomplished.” But 
no matter the success that I was experiencing in  

my skating, I still felt like there was that 
void not being full and fulfilled in my life.  

And this is when I think the Lord began to put 
a couple of skateboarders in my life that were  

Christians. And I remember one night specifically, 
I was out skating with a friend and I knew that  

he was a Christian, and there was something 
different about him. I saw in him a joy and a  

peace that wasn’t dependent on circumstance. His 
confession as a Christian lined up with his life.  

And I knew from being around him that I professed 
Christianity, but I didn’t actually possess it.  

I was not walking with the Lord. And I believe 
it was through this interaction with a friend  

that God started to draw me, and I immediately 
felt convicted over my sin. And I knew that for  

so many years I had been looking to skateboarding 
to be my Savior and not Christ. Around this point  

in my life, I’m seeing friends that I grew up 
with, who grew up in the church, and a lot of  

them are falling away or deconstructing, and so 
I oftentimes would find myself at this overlook,  

just praying to God and asking Him to reassure me. 
I was wrestling with these questions of how can I  

truly believe that the Bible is the Word of God? 
How can I know that this is truth? And I’d spend  

hours up there, trying to have assurance that what 
I believed was truth. My brother introduced me to  

Ligonier. I’m wanting to grow in my relationship 
with the Lord, and I’m wanting to dig deeper into  

Scripture. And so, during my work and during 
my skating, I was listening to R.C. Sproul and  

listening to these guys who are expounding on 
the Word of God and teaching the Word of God  

faithfully. In 2017, I got asked to be a part of a 
skate ministry event, and I had no idea what skate  

ministry even was. I had never heard of it before. 
But now I was seeing a way where skateboarding  

could be used as a tool to reach people with the 
gospel and to just build relationships with these  

skaters. And it’s actually where I met my wife, 
was at this event. After Emma and I get married  

and we’re growing in the Lord together, we were 
really benefiting from the resources that Ligonier  

has. And at this point we started a weekly Bible 
study at our house, which we’ve been doing for  

years now. And, it’s just been a great opportunity 
to have skateboarders and their families come  

to our home every week. So, to be able to have 
Ligonier material to equip other skateboarders  

who might be wrestling with these things, and 
for newer believers, it has just been a joy,  

and I’ve seen just such a positive impact among 
the members. So, for right now, skating, it’s  

still a part of my life, but there’s a different 
approach that I take to it. These younger kids who  

are starting to get into skating, a lot of times 
we come alongside them and pour into them not only  

the gospel, but also just what does the Christian 
life look like? The Lord has given us each a gift  

and each a talent that other people don’t have. 
God has given you unique gifts and talents and  

abilities to use for His purposes. If God can use 
a piece of wood on four wheels for His purposes,  

then He can use whatever He has given you and your 
talents and your abilities for the gospel. I once  

was a skateboarder who was lost, and just through 
the simple act of a Christian skater reaching out  

to me and building a bridge and a friendship and 
showing that he was different in the skateboard  

world, I understood the gospel. I understood that 
Christ bore my sin, that He became sin on the  

cross for me, and that, through faith in Him, 
I could have the very righteousness of Christ  

credited to my account, to be forgiven, and to 
be redeemed and to be made right in His sight.
