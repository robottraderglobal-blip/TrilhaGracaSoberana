been given, we are told, even in these lectures, 
we have the opportunity and the joy of being able  

to serve and give with generosity. And we are 
moved to proclaim. Here, after this conference,  

you will be scattered throughout the world. 
I love saying that, because in this case,  

it's actually real. You will be scattered by the 
Lord, launched throughout the world to declare the  

name of Jesus loudly and lift up the name of 
Christ Jesus on high. Simply Him we proclaim,  

we are told. And we want Jesus' name to be 
known on our lips as well as in our lives.  

And we want all to come to the saving 
knowledge of Jesus Christ, to know and  

receive the blessings that only Christ can lead 
us to as we approach the throne of God's grace.

Are we surprised then that the last command to 
His disciples Jesus left was simply this: "All  

authority in heaven and on earth has been given to 
me," He says. "Go therefore and make disciples of  

all nations, baptizing them in the name of the 
Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit,  

teaching them to observe all that I have commanded 
you. And behold, I am with you always to the close  

of the age." I know all of you are here, myself 
included, because at some point someone heard  

the call and went. And that's what happened 
in Korea since the 1880s, where my parents,  

and the parents before, three generations before, 
came to the knowledge of Jesus Christ. And here I  

am, and I know here you are, because someone went 
and declared and lifted up the name of Jesus. And  

this is what He says, "Go therefore, proclaim 
the name of Jesus," but not without a promise.  

The promise is simply this, "I am with you always 
to the close of the age." "I am with you always,  

until the close of the age. I am with you always," 
Jesus said, "until the world comes to an end."

I heard that when Dr. Ferguson retired from 
his church, he preached from Hebrews 13,  

where the reminder is "Jesus Christ is the 
same yesterday, today and forevermore." And so,  

when He says, "I am with you forever," 
that promise is not just to the disciples  

gathered then; the promise is to us, and 
this promise cannot change. "I am with  

you until the end," because Jesus Christ is 
the same yesterday, today and forevermore.

You and I, in Jesus, are blessed, and as people 
who are blessed, we are proclaimed and declared  

and sent to bless others whom we see. How do 
we end our time when such rich teachings have  

been provided, such rich fellowship has encouraged 
us, as well as enlarged our hearts as the service  

with one another has provided for us hope as we 
serve the Lord together? Here, I can't think of  

a better way than to end with the prayer that we 
began with, which is the benediction of the Word,  

the good Word of God, to remind us of the 
Lord's call, His mission, His message and  

His declaration to you when He says, friends, 
"The Lord (small caps) bless you and keep you."

This is what happens when you turn fifty. 
Sorry. "The Lord (small caps) bless you  

and keep you. The Lord (small caps) make His 
face to shine upon you and be gracious to you.  

The Lord (small caps) lift up his countenance upon 
you and give you peace both now and forevermore."

Let's pray.  

What glorious joy it is for brothers and sisters 
to dwell in unity, for us to sit together in  

reflection upon Your Word, to hear Your Word 
faithfully declared, fellowship with like-minded  

Christians around the globe whom we hadn't known 
before, but now know as brothers and sisters in  

Christ Jesus, our Lord, and to know that we 
are clothed in the holiness and righteousness  

of Christ Jesus, our Lord, undeserving sinners, 
yet now standing before You, being able to say,  

"Our Father in heaven," no longer simply in 
fear calling You our judge, but rushing into  

the very presence of Your grace because of 
our Lord and Savior, Jesus Christ, our Lord.  

Thank You for opportunities like this, for 
You have prepared us for such a time as this,  

for us to sit and know and grow in Your 
graces. And as we are dispersed and scattered,  

recognizing, O Lord, that some of us may never 
see each other again on this side of glory,  

but yet now forever bound by the blood 
of Christ Jesus, who brings us together,  

we ask that You remind us daily how blessed we are 
because of Christ Jesus, our Lord. And knowing and  

experiencing our blessing in Christ Jesus, may 
we lift up praises and thanksgiving to You daily,  

every hour, O Lord, for we know, no matter what 
the circumstances and the environment may be,  

Your promise to go before us and be with us 
is truly certain, as we read upon Your word  

and as You come to us. And, Lord, allow us to 
become Your instruments of grace, that both in  

terms of our teaching and declarations, as well 
as our lives, may declare and lift up the name  

of Jesus Christ on high, for the whole world to 
know, for many to call upon You along with us,  

Lord and Savior, becoming brothers 
and sisters in Christ Jesus, our Lord.

Would You be with my brothers and sisters 
that are here. Go with them. Go before them,  

surrounding them, protect them, O Lord, wherever 
they go. May they never know a day apart from  

Your gracious presence and hand upon their 
lives. May they know and see and experience  

Your power in their ministries as well as in 
their lives. For we pray all these things in  

the kind and gracious and blessed name of 
Your Son, Jesus Christ, our Lord. Amen.
