# The Problem of Guilt: Ultimately with R.C. Sproul

SPROUL: Several years ago, I had a friend who was 
a psychiatrist. Very seriously, he came to me on  

one occasion and asked me to come to work for 
him. I said, “You must be joking. I don’t know  

the first thing about psychiatry. I’m certainly 
not qualified or capable to work in your office,  

dealing with people who are in therapy.” He says, 
“Oh, but you are.” I said, “Why is that?” He said,  

“Because the vast majority of the problems 
that I have to deal with as a psychiatrist  

are all bound up with guilt—guilt and its 
consequences, guilt that is paralyzing,  

guilt that is unresolved.” He said, 
“Most of the people I see don’t need  

a psychiatrist. They need a priest. They need to 
understand how to unlock this problem of guilt.”
