CHUA: My name is Linus Chua. I'm a 
pastor in Pilgrim Covenant Church,  

one of the two pastors. Our church is a 
small congregation. We have about 120–130  

in our morning services. We identify 
ourselves as Reformed Presbyterians. So,  

one of the distinctives of our church would 
be that we sing the Psalms unaccompanied.

To the question of a misunderstanding 
or a mistake with regard to the gospel,  

I think one of the mistakes that some people 
make with regard to the gospel is that the  

gospel is for unbelievers only, or maybe for new 
believers, but not really for mature believers or  

seasoned Christians, and that's a mistake. We 
all need the gospel, whether we're young or  

whether we're old, whether we've been in 
the faith for a few years or many years.

A couple of reasons for that. We need the gospel 
to keep us humble. We all have a tendency to be  

proud, and we need the gospel to remind us that 
we are saved not because we are somehow better  

than my neighbor, or that we have somehow become 
more acceptable to God. It's by grace. And then  

secondly, we need the gospel because we sin. 
We all sin. We're not perfected yet. We need  

the comforts of the gospel. We need to again 
and again repent and believe the gospel. And I  

think the third reason why we need the gospel is 
because we need to be guarded against legalism.  

And I think that we have heard wonderful messages 
this morning and this afternoon with regard to  

sanctification, and we need to be careful that we 
don't become legalists. One of the common mistakes  

with regard to sanctification is that we are 
saved by grace, but we are sanctified by works.  

And that's a mistake. We are saved by grace. We 
are sanctified by grace. We are secured by grace.
