The internet has tore all that down. But you see, 
this is intended to build up the congregation,  

not to be passive receivers: you, that 
you would just listen and obey us—that  

you would come to Christ and that you would be 
built up and that you would use your gifts and  

that you would grow in the edification of the

The Reformation recovered this priesthood of 
all believers. We're not hierarchical bishops,  

untouchable, who you don't know and you can't 
get through the staff to see. We're not CEOs,  

managerial guys managing the treasury. We're not 
vision setters to build our names. We're servants.

We have to fight that our whole life. 
That's why the disciples fell into this,  

et apart to give Christ to 
you, to demonstrate this:  

"When He saw the crowds, He had compassion on 
them, because they were harassed and helpless,  

like sheep without a shepherd." The gospel He 
was giving to them: "The Son of Man came not  

to be served, but to serve and give His 
life a ransom for many." Do you see it?  

That was greatness—going to a cross for you 
and rising triumphant and sending servants

The truly great one made Himself nothing,  

and becoming a slave served all the 
way to the cross to forgive your sins,  

my friends, and He gives servants to 
minister that same forgiveness to you.

"Many things are undoubtedly contained in the 
gospel," says Calvin, "but the chief aim which  

God intends to accomplish by the gospel is the 
forgiveness of sins," John 6, his commentary;  

John 20. This is what church government recovered. 
This is what the Reformation recovered in church  

government: God gave us servants who shepherd in 
His name, and that is greatness in the kingdom.

So, I close then with this thought: Do 
you have a problem submitting to that?  

I don't think so. As office bearers, we need 
to practice—this is my challenge to anyone who  

listens to me myself—to practice what is true 
greatness in serving the flock. But from you,  

if there is to be any Reformation in the 
church today, we need to ask you, humbly,

is true greatness found? Where are you looking

church, where Christ put that in place for you,  

where your children will be saved by the

If there is to be a Reformation of the church

again for the reforming of church government.
