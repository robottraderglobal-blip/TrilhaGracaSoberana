# A Heavenly Helper: Who Is the Holy Spirit? with Sinclair Ferguson

FERGUSON: Our last study together, we were thinking
about that marvelous teaching that the Lord

Jesus gave to Nicodemus, recorded in John
chapter 3, on the new birth. And we were thinking

about the origin of that birth. We were thinking
of the sovereign bestowal of it. We were thinking

about the marvelous effects of it, how it
transforms us.

And, just at the end, you remember, we were
thinking about the necessity of the new birth.

Jesus emphasizes, "Nicodemus, you must be
born again." Now, the question I want to take

up in this study is -- so, when we are born
again, how does the Holy Spirit continue his

ministry to us? He brings us to new life.
But what does he meant to us, now that we

have been born again?

I want for our study now to turn again to
those marvelous verses we've looked at and

come back to time and time again in the second
half of John's gospel, chapter 14. It's sometimes

said that Luke is the gospel writer who is
most interested in the Holy Spirit. It really

would be truer to say John is the gospel writer
who is most interested in the Holy Spirit,

but even truer to say the Lord Jesus is the
person who is most interested in the ministry

of the Holy Spirit.

You remember the words that we've returned
to again and again: "I will ask the Father,"

John 14:16, "and He will give you another
helper, another parakletos." Parakletos is

the word, it comes from the preposition 'para'
and the verb 'kaleo' -- to call alongside.

Jesus is going to give us the Holy Spirit
to enable us in our lives, as the one who

is called alongside us to be our helper. It's
a beautiful picture, isn't it?

We can think about Jesus giving the Holy Spirit
in terms of being baptized with the Spirit,

but what a beautiful picture this is when
Jesus calls the Holy Spirit alongside believers,

to minister to them, to minister in them.
But notice, even more carefully, the words

that Jesus uses. "I will ask the Father, and
He will give you another helper." In classical

Greek, there are two words that are used that
can be translated, in English, by 'another.'

One of them means 'another of a different
kind'. The other word means 'another of the

same kind.' That difference doesn't always
pertain the use of the Greek language, but

I think it does, actually, pertain here. Jesus
says, "I will give you another paraclete.

I will give you another helper." The word
'another' here means 'another of the same

kind.'

And this is one of the ways in which Jesus
is helping His disciples. They are distressed

because He's going to leave. They can't understand
what He says later on, "It's to your advantage

that I go away, otherwise the helper will
not come to you." But here, He's giving us

a clue as to how it is to their advantage,
and to ours, that Jesus has gone away and

sent the Holy Spirit to us. Because the Holy
Spirit is another helper, a helper, a minister

to us, of the same kind as the Lord Jesus.

Now, if you just grasp that thought for a
moment, and I want to try and show you, from

John 14, how it is that the Holy Spirit comes
to be another of the same kind of helper to

us as was the Lord Jesus. Here are the disciples
thinking, "We're going to lose because Jesus

is going from us. Nothing could take Jesus'
place." And Jesus is actually saying, "Yes,

someone will take my place, and he will be
another helper of the same kind."

I want to show this in three different ways.
First of all, because Jesus tells us the Holy

Spirit will come to be our teacher. "The Holy
Spirit," He says, in verse 26, "whom the Father

will send in my name, he will teach you."
I wonder if you remember, in the previous

chapter, when Jesus had washed the disciples'
feet. He had concluded the foot washing with

some teaching.

Chapter 13, verse 12. "When He had washed
their feet and put on His outer garments and

resumed His place, he said to them, 'Do you
understand what I've done to you? You call

me teacher and Lord, for you are right, for
so I am. If I, then, your Lord and teacher.'"

That's what they were going to miss. For these
three years, Jesus had unfolded the secrets

of the kingdom of God.

They'd never heard teaching like it. He spoke
with authority, and not as the usual teachers.

They'd heard such amazing things from His
lips about the kingdom of God and about what

it meant to have God as your heavenly Father.
And now, their teacher was leaving them. They

were desperate.

"But," says Jesus, "when the Spirit comes,
he's going to take my place, and he will be

your teacher." But in what sense will the
Holy Spirit be our teacher? Well, for one

thing, as Jesus makes clear during the course
of His exposition here, the Holy Spirit will

teach us what Paul calls 'the deep things
of God.' It's very interesting how Jesus brings

this out in these verses when He begins to
teach us that, "When the Spirit comes," He

says, "you will know that I am in my Father
and that you are in me, and that I am in you."

Just absolutely amazing to see some of the
things that He says. The Spirit will teach

us. Look at verse 31. "The world will know
that I love the Father. The Spirit will teach

us other things." Jesus speaks about the way
in which, through the Spirit, we come to see

the amazing unity of fellowship and love that
there is between the Father and the Son and

the Son and the Father. And so, when the Spirit
comes, he will inwardly teach us marvelous

things about God.

You know, it's very interesting to me that
most people seem to think that the doctrine

of the Trinity is the most speculative and
least practical doctrine of the Christian

faith, and it leaves them very cold. But when
the Spirit comes, and when the Spirit shows

you the love the Father has for the Son, and
the love the Son has for the Father, you are

in awe and filled with worship. And in a way,
Jesus is saying, "Rather than know me less,

when I go and the Spirit comes, you will actually
begin to discover things about me that have

never really dawned on you."

So part of the Spirit's ministry is to lead
us into worship of the Father and the Son,

to love the Father because He loves the Son,
to love the Son because He obeys the Father,

and to be able to say, as the apostle John
would later say, "with an astonishment of

Spirit." Our fellowship is with the Father
and with His Son. So when the Spirit comes,

he reveals the deep things of God, but when
the Spirit comes, he also reveals the great

heights of grace.

Look at what Jesus says here in Chapter 14,
for example in verse 20. "In that day when

the Spirit comes, you will know that I am
in my Father, and you are in me, and I am

in you." I'll never forget the day, Sunday
morning, when, as a young Christian -- I was

probably 15. I'd been a Christian, I'm sure,
for just a matter of months, and my minister

preached on the words in Colossians chapter
1: "Christ, in you, the hope of glory."

For the first time in my life, I heard a message
about the New Testament's teaching that when

we become Christians through the Holy Spirit,
the Lord Jesus Christ comes and dwells within

us. Now, I was a very shy 15-year-old boy,
I think probably quite interned. And, I didn't

dance. But I remember lingering behind in
church that morning, walking up the road,

turning the corner into the street where I
lived, and, very self-consciously, looking

up and down the street, looking at the windows
in the street to make sure absolutely nobody

was watching me. I danced home.

I just couldn't believe it. The Lord of glory
would come and dwell in my heart. Of course,

I didn't understand all of this teaching,
but I see now, as I read this passage, that

was the Holy Spirit. That was why it was good
for me that Jesus went away in order to give

me the Spirit, because I would have thought
if Jesus leaves me, I'll know less about Him.

And the truth of the matter is, because Jesus
has sent the Holy Spirit, our relationship

to Him is so much more intimate.

Now, how does the Holy Spirit do this? Well,
in this farewell discourse, Jesus gives us

further clues. He says, "You know, when the
Spirit comes," chapter 14, verse 26, "He will

teach you all things and bring to your remembrance
all that I have said." Then, in chapter 15,

verse 26, "When the helper comes, whom I will
send to you from the Father, the Spirit of

truth, he will bear witness about me. He will
tell you the things that are to come."

Now, all of those statements that run together
really boil down to this. Jesus is preparing

the apostles to give the Holy Spirit in the
Scriptures, the teaching of God's Spirit in

the New Testament Scriptures, given to the
church. The Spirit will remind them of everything

Jesus did and said. The Spirit will lead them
into all truth. The Spirit will show them

the things that are to come -- that's the
New Testament Scriptures.

And actually, that's how we get this teaching,
don't we? None of us is there in the upper

room. This teaching doesn't fall down from
heaven. We don't make it up ourselves. Where

do we learn the teaching of the Holy Spirit?
We learn the teaching of the Holy Spirit through

the New Testament Scriptures.

And so, Jesus, here, is preparing us to understand
that if we want to receive the teaching ministry

of the Holy Spirit, we look to the Scriptures,
because there, we learn about the Lord Jesus

Christ. There's something else here. I'm sure
some of you are teachers, maybe a teacher

in Sunday School. Maybe you've been a teacher
in an elementary school or a high school and

the worst part of the year, as a theological
teacher, for me, you can probably imagine,

is grading at the end of the semester.

And you're reading a student's paper or his
exam, and you go to yourself, "Am I such a

bad teacher as this?" You read another and
you think, "This is terrible." Then, you read

one, and you think, "This guy really got it.
I'm not so bad after all. I wish I could get

inside the head of these other students. I
wish I could go inside their head and sort

out their heads, but I know I can't do that.
All I've got is words."

But you see, Jesus is saying when the Spirit
teaches us, he's not only got words in the

Scriptures, but he dwells in our hearts. And
he's able to work from within to illumine

our understanding, so that instead of -- this
is the marvelous thing. Of course, they couldn't

understand it at this point. "Oh, Jesus, we're
going to miss your teaching!"

Jesus is saying, "No, when the Spirit comes,
you're going to understand my teaching at

last." So, the Spirit comes to us in order
to be our teacher. Second, the Spirit comes

to us in order to be our counselor, or in
order to be counsel to us. Now, Jesus had

given them counsel, hadn't he? That was a
great part of His ministry. And now, they

are concerned he's going. They're going to
miss His counsel. They feel, "I'm not going

to be able to say to Jesus, 'Jesus, explain
this to me.'" I wonder if you've noticed,

as you've read through John 13 through 16,
they're always asking Him questions and He's

always patiently explaining things to them.
And they're going to miss His counsel.

But you notice what He says in chapter 14,
verse 23. He says, "If anyone loves me, they

will keep my word and my Father will love
him and we will come to him and make our home

with him through the Holy Spirit and they
will receive the counsel of the Son and the

Father." Now, this word paraclete -- paracletos
in Greek -- is a word that's very elastic

in its meaning. One of its meanings is a legal
meaning, which is why some of the translations

translate the word paracletos as counselor
or counsel, as we would say.

You know, you find yourself in trouble and
you need legal counsel. You may know older

lawyers who are now that they are no longer
active partners in the firm, and they've got

these words 'of counsel' in brackets behind
their name. And they're advocates. And they

will take up your case and they will argue
it out.

This is what Jesus is saying about the Holy
Spirit. All through their companionship with

Jesus, Jesus has defended them. Jesus has
been alongside them. Jesus has really plead

their case. And now, He's saying to them,
"When the Spirit comes, he's going to be your

counsel."

But the interesting thing is this. One of
the differences between this world and our

world is you find yourself in trouble with
the law, then you need to go to Downtown Orlando

and look for a nice brass plate that says
something like 'McDonald, Smith, and Ferguson,

Attorneys at Law.' And you need to check with
your bank manager that you're going to be

able to have enough in the bank to pay their
bills. You need professional help.

That wasn't the case in terms of the way in
which law was enacted in Jesus' time. If you

were in trouble and you were to be brought
along to the court, you didn't go down to

the local law office, you went to your best
friend, to the person who had been with you

longest, who knew you best, who'd be able
to bear witness in court and say, "Your Lordship,"

or, "Your Honor," depending on what country
you live in, "He could never have done this.

I know him. And here are the facts of the
matter. Here is the evidence."

And so, you would call in your best friend,
who had known you longest, to be your counsel,

your advocate, your witness. Now, think about
what we've learned about the Holy Spirit.

Look at John chapter 15, in the last verse.
Jesus says, "When the helper comes, when the

counselor comes, whom I'll send to you from
the Father, the Spirit of truth who proceeds

from the Father, he will bear witness about
me. And you also will bear witness."

Now, notice these words, "Because you've been
with me from the beginning." In a sense, they're

going to be counsel to Jesus in the world.
The world's going to prosecute Jesus, but

they're going to stand up and say, "I know
the truth about Jesus." And they're going

to be witnesses you can trust, because they've
been with Jesus from the beginning. They're

going to be able to say, "I saw it with my
own eyes. I heard it with my own ears."

Now, do you see the parallel between what
Jesus is saying to the apostles, and what

He's saying about the Holy Spirit? The Holy
Spirit is Jesus' counselor in our lives and

our counsel, because He has been with Jesus
from the beginning. I must be getting old,

but allow me to tell you another story of
my childhood today. We didn't have a television

set until, I think, I was probably 11 or 12.

But every second Saturday night, my family
was gathered together and we were taken across

to the other side of the city we lived in,
to my aunt and uncle and my two cousins, because

they had a television set. My brother and
I were put into a room with our two female

cousins and given candies to eat and chocolate
and drinks and all the rest of it. And then,

at 8:00 PM, we would be brought through to
the main room where the television set was

on, so we could all watch Perry Mason.

So I was brought up on the original, black
and white Perry Masons -- with Perry Mason

and Della Street and Paul Drake. And those
of you who remember, at those, they always

ended the same way, didn't they? Whoever it
was that Perry was defending, it just looked

as though the case was going to be lost. And
then, just at the last moment, at about five

minutes to 9:00 PM, the court doors would
open and in would come the detective, Paul

Drake, and he'd give Perry the wink, you know?

And then, they would be brought into the court
-- the really key witness. And, Hamilton Burger,

who was the DA -- I always used to think,
"There's something about that name, Ham Burger,

that must be, you know, really distinctly
American." You know, he would look so sheepish,

and the case would be dismissed.

Jesus is saying the Holy Spirit is the key
witness. And he's going to come, and although

you'll face opposition and difficulty, and
although you'll see that my name is maligned

in the world, my Holy Spirit will be my advocate
and your advocate. He will be your defense

attorney. And in my name, he will also be
the prosecuting attorney, because he will

convict the world of sin and righteousness
and judgment and bring them to faith in me.

So, what a picture this is of the ministry
of the Holy Spirit. They fear they're going

to lose and miss Jesus' ministry. But He's
going to continue to be their teacher. He's

going to continue to be their counselor. And
then, the third word I want to use about Jesus'

teaching on the Holy Spirit may make you think
I've been in the United States too long. He

is the teacher. He is the counselor.

But Jesus also describes him as the divine
homemaker. Notice how this 14th chapter in

John's gospel begins. It begins by describing
Jesus as a homemaker. "I'm going to leave

you, but I'm going to make a place ready for
you in my Heavenly Father's house." Jesus,

in that sense, is gone to be the homemaker,
get the home ready for us, just the way some

of you may say to your family, at the end
of morning church, "I need to go home to get

things ready for our guests."

That's what homemakers do. But now, look at
what Jesus says the Holy Spirit will do. He

says in chapter 14, verse 18, "I'll not leave
you as orphans. I'll come to you." And again,

in Verse 21, "He who loves me will be loved
by my Father, and I will love him and manifest

myself to him." And again, in verse 23, "If
anyone loves me, he will keep my word, and

my Father will love him, and we will come
to him." He means, through the Holy Spirit.

"We will come to him and make our home with
him."

I think in that one thought we can almost
summarize the ministry of the Holy Spirit.

The Holy Spirit works in our lives to make
our lives a home for the Father and the Son

to live in. That explains all the joys of
the Christian life. That explains the challenges

and difficulties of the Christian life, because
the Holy Spirit is transforming us so that

we'll be a home for the Father and the Son.

I love those moments when I'm with my wife,
we're introduced to somebody, and they say,

"Oh, so you're Dorothy's husband?" Because
she's so used to being the homemaker and being

introduced, and people say, "Oh, so you're
Sinclair's wife?" Homemakers tend to be anonymous,

don't they? Actually, in our world, sadly,
they're sometimes demeaned.

But if you're in the home, if you're part
of the family, everything depends on the homemaker.

It's the homemaker who makes the place home
for the Father and for the Son. So what a

priceless description Jesus is giving His
disciples. "You will not lose out because

I am leaving you, because when I leave you,
I'll send the Holy Spirit to be your teacher

through my word, to be your counselor through
your life, and to be the homemaker to transform

your life so that I and my Father will enjoy
living in your heart."

No wonder we say, "Thank you Jesus, for giving
us the Holy Spirit." Let us pray. Lord Jesus,

you could give us no greater gift than to
give us your own Holy Spirit. We thank you

for all he teaches us about you and for all

you. We love your Father because of Your Spirit,
and we want to say today that we love you,

Holy Spirit, because you show us the Father
